A little bit about me

This is me

I'm a 26 years old guy from Sandnes, who currently studies to be a frontend developer, at Noroff. In my spare time i like to work out, play video games or hang out with friends in the weekends. I also like to build Pc whenever i have the chance to, or someone needs help with upgrading pc parts. My passion for Pc in general has grown over the years. I also love music, so every summer i have to visit some festivals outside Norway with other friends, who shares the same interest in music genre. I am more of the social type of person, and like to spend time with friends and meet others, either if is at a party or playing sports outside.

My career so far

To begin with i started out as a carpenter trainee when i was around 17-18years, which I quickly found out was not for me. After two years in this industry, I got back to school again. I took something called påbygg, which is something you need to take to study for a higher degree's like vocational school, bachelor, master and so on. After this i took a year off and worked as a cashier for about one year. After that i went back to school to improve my grade's, and switched job as well. The job i got then is the job I still have, and that is as an health care worker. Everything from patient's with schizophrenia, to older people that just needs help with getting dressed. After working with this for five years, i wanted to change direction, and a friend of me recommended me trying frontend development. I signed up at Noroff, and a few months later i now started my journey as a frontend developer. I had mostly no idea what this was, and had zero experience in this field, but so far i enjoy this study/proffesion. I am pretty excited to see were this is going, and i hope this is something i can do as a proffesion for years to come.

Why i chose frontend development

As i mentioned earlier, i got this proffesion recommended by a friend that studies to become one as well. I looked it up and saw that frontend developer was a sough-after proffesion, and also a well paid one. So i gave it a shot and started applying to Noroff. What was great was that you did not need any experience within the proffesion to start this course. But to be honest, it has been rough and a lot to learn. Coding is difficult, but it helps that it is much help online to get from others. I dont regret my choice choosing to do this, because i enjoy it, and it has been fun so far. I am curious to see where this takes me in the future.
